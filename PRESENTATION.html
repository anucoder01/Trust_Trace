TrustTrace - Final Presentation Notes

Private notes for project demonstration. Do not share publicly.

Slide 1: Introduction & The Problem
  • Hook: Start with the financial cost of signature forgery (billions lost annually globally).
  • Problem: Traditional static verification is easily fooled by skilled forgers. Tellers rely on human eye, which cannot detect micro-hesitations or temporal biometric data.
  • Solution: TrustTrace. A 5-module AI intelligence platform combining Computer Vision, Temporal Analysis, and Risk Context.
Slide 2: System Architecture Overview
  • Briefly explain the 5 modules. Emphasize that it's a layered defense.
  • Key Tech Stack to mention: OpenCV for static analysis, PyTorch/LSTMs for temporal, Scikit-Learn (SVM) for classical CV baseline, and HTML/JS/CSS for the interactive frontend.
  • Dataset: Mention we use the standard CEDAR or Robinreni Kaggle dataset (Genuine vs Forged) for baseline CV training.
Slide 3: Module 1 & 2 - Static Analysis (CV)
  • Demo Action: Show the "Micro-Tremor" drawing pad.
  • Talking Points: Explain that HOG (Histogram of Oriented Gradients) captures the structure of the signature. SVM classifies it.
  • Mention "Micro-tremors": Forgers hesitate. The pen stops, causing ink blotting. The lines jitter. The CV pipeline detects this deviation from a smooth ballistic curve.
Slide 4: Module 3 - Dynamic Stylus Tracking
  • Demo Action: Sign on the "Stylus Track" pad.
  • Talking Points: This is the game-changer. We don't just look at the final image; we look at how it was drawn.
  • Velocity, Pressure, Sequence, Lift Timing. An LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory network) learns this unique "biometric rhythm". It's near impossible to fake both the image and the speed/rhythm perfectly.
Slide 5: Module 4 & 5 - Adaptive Learning & Teller View
  • Adaptive Learning: Explain "Concept Drift". People's signatures change as they age or get injured. Our system updates the baseline centroid so we don't start rejecting genuine signatures (reducing false positives).
  • Teller Dashboard: Walk through the final real-world use case. Upload a cheque, get the result.
  • Demo Action: Click the "Verify Cheque" button and show the Grad-CAM heatmap. Explain that the heatmap provides explainability to the teller (showing exactly *where* the forgery is suspected).
Q&A Prep - Expected Questions
  • Q: What happens if the system is down? A: We have the classical SVM model as a lightweight fallback that can run locally.
  • Q: How do you handle different pen types/colors? A: Pre-processing (grayscaling, thresholding, L2-Hys normalization in HOG) makes the model invariant to ink color and minor thickness variations.
  • Q: What is the accuracy? A: Base CV model achieves ~90-95% on standard datasets. With temporal data fused, we aim for >99%.
